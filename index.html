History

cotillion
  • formal ball as a venue for presenting debutantes
  • social dance that began in early 18th century by french royalty
  • upon the early 20th century affluent black families began to adopt the tradition
  • girls were often intoduced to society which included the family's social and business network
  • 1940s black fraternities, sororities, civic and social organizations formed the social structure of the day
